Spring Water

This water comes from natural springs and is filtered for purity.

One of the healthiest and most refreshing water you can drink

It is generally safe to drink spring water every day, especially if it comes from a reputable and well-regulated source

Spring water is considered the best drinking water to drink

Spring water is naturally filtered from the earth

Spring water is unique in that it is naturally filtered and contains essential minerals

Well Water

Extracted from deep within the earth, well water is rich in minerals.

Approximately 25% of global rainwater becomes groundwater, which supplies wells

If there is proper research and preparation, most wells never run dry. Medium to deep wells last two to three decades, at minimum

The deeper the well the less likely it is that contamination will occur

Well water is the best thing to drink for your body

Dams

Extracted from deep within the earth, well water is rich in minerals.

Damn is a well-like structure that is built across a creek or river, to block the flow of water through the landscape

When it rains, water builds up behind the dam, creating an artificial lake or reservoir

After water treatment, it can be used for drinking. This can help ensure that people are drinking pure water, preventing diseases like cholera.

Streams

A spring is the result of an aquifer being filled to the point that the water overflows onto the land surface

Spring water can be developed into drinking water supply by collecting the discharged water using the tile or pipe and running the water into some type of sanitary storage tank

Spring water provides much-needed oxygen to the body and the brain

Spring water aids in good digestion, helps maintain a healthy weight, and tastes great

Spring water never tastes flat or boiled

It makes an excellent drinking experience and one that does wonders for the body

Rains

Rainwater is one of the purest sources of water available.

Its quality almost always exceeds that of ground or surface water because it does not come in contact with soil or rocks where it can dissolve minerals and salts.

Most rain is perfectly safe to drink and may be even cleaner than the public water supply.

Only rain that has fallen directly from the sky should be collected for drinking.
